Did you know? Kauai is the oldest of the main Hawaiian islands (about 5 million years old!) and gets so much rain that it's home to one of the wettest spots on Earth — Mount Wai'ale'ale. That's why everything is SO incredibly green!

Things To Do on Kauai

Adventures for the whole family on the Garden Isle

🏞

Waimea Canyon

The "Grand Canyon of the Pacific" — a 3,600-foot-deep canyon painted in red, orange, and green. Drive to the lookouts, hike the easy Iliau Nature Loop, and prepare for your jaws to drop!

🛶

Mountain Tubing Adventure

Float through pitch-black lava-rock tunnels from the 1870s on inner tubes with headlamps! A 2.5-mile underground river adventure through an old sugar plantation. Ages 5+ welcome.

🐦

Kilauea Lighthouse

Walk to a clifftop lighthouse where you can spot massive seabirds with 6-foot wingspans! December is also prime whale-watching season from the cliffs. Kids can earn a Junior Ranger badge!

⛵

Na Pali Coast Boat Tour

Cruise along 4,000-foot sea cliffs, hidden waterfalls, and sea caves — with a great chance of spotting humpback whales! Winter tours depart from Port Allen on the south shore.

🏖

Hanalei Bay Beach Day

One of the most beautiful bays on Earth! Build sandcastles on the wide golden beach, play in the shallows near the pier, and watch surfers ride the big winter waves. Bring a picnic!

🦈

Snorkeling at Anini Beach

Protected by a long offshore reef, Anini Beach stays calm even in winter — the North Shore's best bet for safe snorkeling, wading, and paddle sports with the family.

⚠️
Winter Safety Tip: The North Shore gets big waves in December! Tunnels Beach and Queen's Bath are beautiful but dangerous in winter swells. Enjoy them from a safe viewing distance. For swimming, stick to Anini Beach or head to Poipu on the south shore.

Did you know? December is peak big-wave season on the North Shore! The famous Banzai Pipeline and Sunset Beach host world-championship surf competitions with waves reaching 30+ feet. You can watch from the sand — it's absolutely thrilling!

Things To Do on the North Shore

Big waves, sea turtles, shrimp trucks & Christmas magic

🏄

Watch Pipeline & Sunset Beach

Stand on the sand and watch surfers ride some of the most powerful waves on Earth! Free, no tickets needed — just show up at Ehukai Beach Park and prepare to yell "WHOAAAA!"

🎭

Polynesian Cultural Center

Explore six island villages — throw spears, husk coconuts, paddle canoes, and get a Keiki Passport! The evening "Ha: Breath of Life" fire-knife show will leave everyone speechless.

🦐

Shrimp Trucks!

Giovanni's famous graffiti-covered truck serves legendary garlic butter shrimp over rice. Get messy, love every bite, and sign the truck with a Sharpie like thousands of visitors before you!

🦈

Snorkeling at Kuilima Cove

This naturally protected cove stays calm even when the rest of the North Shore is raging — snorkel with tropical fish, sea turtles, and maybe an octopus! Right at Turtle Bay.

🐢

Laniakea "Turtle Beach"

Hawaiian green sea turtles regularly haul themselves onto the sand to sunbathe. Watch these gentle giants up close (from a respectful distance) — it's absolutely magical!

🐎

Horseback Riding to Kawela Bay

Ride horses along the coastline through Turtle Bay's 1,300-acre property to a hidden turquoise cove. Family-friendly wagon rides also available — one of the most beautiful secret spots on the island!

Disney Magic Awaits! Aulani has the only private saltwater snorkeling lagoon on Oahu called Rainbow Reef, where you can swim alongside over 1,000 tropical fish! Plus, Mickey Mouse personally visits your table at the Makahiki character breakfast.

Things To Do at Aulani & Ko Olina

Disney magic meets Hawaiian paradise

🏊

Waikolohe Valley Water Park

An 8,200 sq ft zero-entry pool with the Volcanic Vertical body slide, Tubestone Curl tube ride, a lazy river through caverns & fountain springs, and Menehune Bridge splash zone for little ones!

🌍

Aunty's Beach House (Free Kids Club!)

Ages 4-12 get a supervised "beach mansion" packed with Disney movies, Hawaiian crafts, video games, and themed activities. Complimentary with your resort stay — kids will never want to leave!

🐥

Character Meet & Greets

Mickey, Minnie, Moana, Stitch, Goofy & Pluto pop up throughout the resort for photos and hugs! Book the Makahiki character breakfast where Mickey visits your table.

🌞

Ko Olina Lagoon Snorkeling

Wade into calm, crystal-clear protected lagoons where even the youngest kids can safely snorkel over butterflyfish and tangs. Shallow water (4-5 ft), soft imported sand — pure paradise.

🎇

Ka Wa'a Lu'au

Aulani's beachfront luau features a Hawaiian feast, live music, hula dancing, and fire-knife performers under the stars. The perfect way to celebrate your trip!

🦀

Rainbow Reef Snorkeling

Aulani's private saltwater snorkeling lagoon is home to 1,000+ tropical fish! It's the only one on Oahu — like swimming in a real-life aquarium. Gear provided.
